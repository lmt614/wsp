Project Analysis

Site Intent

The main goal I hope to achieve in redesigning Christ Episcopal Church is to bring a focal point to the site, allowing the visitor to be drawn in or interested in the content. I will do this by organizing and condensing the content. I plan to remove unnecessary tables and give more visual hierarchy so that the content is more readable and information is easier to find. I would like to add a logo and create organized navigation. I would also like to implement a CTA (call to action) allowing the user the ability to interact with the interface. For instance, bringing in a newsletter, where they can fill out a form and sign up for updates or events. I would like for this site to have a liquid layout by using responsive type units such as ems and percentages and shifting the content. This will make the website more functional on a wider variety of screen sizes and resolutions. Bringing the site up to standards with HTML5 and CSS3 will give us a faster more efficient site.

Target Audience

Scenarios

Lucy Reynolds is a twenty-one year old college student who is moving from Oregon to Washington. Lucy was raised in a Christian environment and was a faithful member of her home church in Oregon. Now, Lucy has moved to attend college in Washington. After moving and getting settled into her classes it’s now Friday and Lucy is strapped for time. Between her classes she remembers she needs to search for a church to go to on Sunday. Lucy picks up her cell phone and begins to search for Christian Episcopal Churches in Seattle.

Ms. Jackson is a single parent to twin boys. After a harrowing experience of a broken marriage and years of never caring about God, Mrs. Jackson is looking to get her life back together and raise her children in church. She sits down at her computer and begins surfing the internet. Ms. Jackson is looking for a church that has a bus route because she is a struggling single mom with no car and very limited income.

Mr. Smith is a sixty year old wealthy business man who is a devout Christian. Mr. Smith has been in Washington all of his life and is looking to give a donation to a church that has good moral and Biblical beliefs. He gets on his computer at work and decides to type in the church website address he received from a friend. He types it in and it opens. He wants to get in contact with the minister of the church but has trouble finding the contact information. Frustrated, after searching for ten minutes for the ministers’ phone number he finally locates the number and makes the call.
